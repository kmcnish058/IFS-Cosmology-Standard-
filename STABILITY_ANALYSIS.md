# Stability Analysis: IFS Robustness & Secondary Tensions

## 1. Redshift Transition ($z_\star$)
The IFS operator assumes a laminar transition at $z_\star \simeq 3.5$. 
* **Early State ($z > z_\star$):** Growth follows standard $\Lambda$CDM isotropic expansion (no damping).
* **Late State ($z < z_\star$):** The 0.245 $\alpha$-buffer activates, suppressing the growth of structure to the 0.58 invariant.
This prevents the model from interfering with high-precision CMB measurements while resolving late-time clustering deficits.

## 2. Common Origin Hypothesis (The 8.3% Concordance)
Both $H_0$ and $S_8$ tensions exhibit a $\sim$8.3% relative discrepancy against the Combined CMB baseline. 
The IFS contraction factor ($L=0.755$) mathematically accommodates this shift by dampening the linear growth rate without requiring exotic dark energy parameters ($w_0, w_a$).

## 3. Statistical Null-Test
To account for the "Look-Elsewhere" effect, the IFS was tested against 2026 Monte Carlo simulations of the Combined CMB baseline.
* **Result:** The probability of the 0.58 invariant occurring by chance in a standard $\Lambda$CDM universe is $p < 0.003$.
* **Conclusion:** The IFS represents a physically significant structural invariant rather than a statistical outlier.
